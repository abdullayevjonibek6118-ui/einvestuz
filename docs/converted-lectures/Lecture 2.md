2. Industry barriers: definitions (1/2)
• Joe, S. Bain - barrier exists if a new firm cannot achieve the same
profitability level upon entry as the existing firms had prior to their
market entry
• Later, G. Stigler - asymmetry in the behavior of existing firms versus
firms attempting to enter the market.
• H. Demsetz - identifying differences, or asymmetries, in the capabilities
of insider and outsider firms, primarily reflected in the lower costs
enjoyed by insider firms

2. Industry barriers: definitions (2/2)
Industry barriers are combination of objective and subjective factors that
either prevent an entering firm from establishing profitable operations
within an industry market or hold back its exit from the market without
incurring significant losses.

2. Inefficiently impeded entry
Features
• Competitors may have low cost advantage
• In the long run it is more advantageous
for existing firms to allow new entrants
• In the short term, this often results in a
price deviation from marginal costs for
some firms

Features

• Prices in these markets tend

2. Market models
Market models are combinations of key market characteristics that
determine prices and the production volumes of market participants. The
key characteristics of market models include:
• Entry and exit barriers
• Number of producers and buyers in the market
• Size of producers, their market share, and market power
