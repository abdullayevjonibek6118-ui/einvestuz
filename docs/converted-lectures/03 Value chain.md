3. Value chain: definition
The value chain is a tool for analyzing potential sources to
provide greater product or service value to consumers and
identify synergy effects. The value chain "breaks down a
company's activities into strategically important types. This
allows to study costs and existing and potential means of
their differentiation."
Michael E. Porter

3. Research and development (1/3)
Research and development (R&D) is the series of activities that companies
undertake to innovate. R&D is often the first stage in the development
process that results in market research product development, and product
testing.
The value of R&D
• Innovation and Progress
• Competitive Advantage
• Economic Growth
• Solving Global Challenges

3. Value chain. Life cycle. Investment,
innovation and financial activity.
3. Research and development (2/3)
Main Areas of R&D
• Innovative Products and Services: Development of new products and services that
meet market needs and provide unique advantages for consumers.
• Improvement of Existing Products and Services: Implementation of enhancements
and modifications to existing products and services to improve their quality, functionality,
and appeal to consumers.
• Technological Innovations: Development and implementation of new technologies
that can be used in production, management, and customer service.
• Marketing Research: Studying the market, competitors, and consumer preferences to
determine the most effective strategies for promoting and selling new products and
services.
